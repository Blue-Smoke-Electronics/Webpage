A Portable "Benshtop" Powersupply

blue Smoke Electronics is proud to present a new type of powersuplly. The (P)ortable (B)enchtop (P)owersupply (PBP) is a small size battery driven powersupply with all the fearues you normaly expect in a traditional benchtop powersupply.

Do I need a Benchtop powersupply?

A benchtop powersuply is used by engieers and sientists when they are working with lov voltage DC electricity. There is two features that make a bencthop powersupply so very usefull and that is ajustabel voltage, and ajustable current limit.

As a voltage source

ajustable output voltage means that a powersupply can be used as voltage source, witch is a part of almost all electronic circuits. It also mean that you can use it in place of a battery of any type.

As a current source

when the current limit is applyed the powersupply act as a current source. while not as comon as voltage sources, thay are used in large number of elecotonic ciruits. One example of a current source is needed is when lighting up a LED.

Protection

When testing out new circuits it is comon parctise to set the current limit just abouv the current you are expecting the circuti to draw. That way, if somthing is wrong, the chance of somtihng bing damage by large currents is reduced.

Why portable?

Tradisional benchtop powersupplyes are, as the name sugestes, designed to be stationary at your workbench. For most projects this is fine, but for some project you have to take out a extention cord and a trolly and bring your powersupply out into the feald. To make this process easyere, Blue Smoke electronics has desigend portable vertion. No need to bring a extention cord, and the powersuply fitts in your pocket.

Other benefits of a battery

Not needing to be connected whith a cable all the time is not the only benefit of having having a battery internaly in the device.

Energy buffer

The battery acts a buffer, alowing a constant low current input and high current output burtst. This allows device to be powered from any USB port and deliver power indefenetly to a device that requiers more powerdraw than USB can provide if the powerdraw is not continus. Servo motors are grate examples of devices that require a lot of power when moving, but most of the time do not require any power alowing the battery to charge back up.

True isolation from the grid

For some RF sensitive mesurements, you do realy want to be soure that there is no noice copeled from the powergrid into youre electronics. One realy good way to make sure no signal is coming from the grid is to disconnect from the grid. Not fysicaly bing connected also eliminates the posibility of ground loops.

No risk of electrocution

There is nowhere in the device that the voltage levels are more than a fiew volts higher that the output voltage. This makes it imposible to get a dangures electric shock, eaven if you do somting realy stupid like taking it with you for a swim. Do not use a powersupply connected to 230V when testing equipnent in the baththube.
